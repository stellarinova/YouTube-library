3 Holy Spirit Steps That TRANSFORM My Morning!

Channel: Steve Larson
Published: 20251212
Screens, schedules, and low-grade anxiety steal the quiet we crave. This simple three-step rhythm—mind, Word, prayer—turns distracted mornings into steady companionship with God.

Why Mornings Slip Away

Most of us wake to a mental avalanche: unfinished tasks, stray worries, and an inbox waiting to pounce. The result is spiritual drift—not disbelief, just distraction. A small, repeatable structure can pull us back to center.

Step One: Center the Mind

Start with expressive writing: list what you feel—stress, anticipation, envy, hope. Name it to tame it. Follow with gratitude (specific is terrific), then reframe challenges using “five alternative interpretations” to see a situation from fresh angles and ask which view aligns with Scripture. Close by reflecting on yesterday: What was meaningful? What did I learn? What would I do differently?

Step Two: Read—and Record—the Word

Let Scripture speak first. Jot short insights in the margin; save longer reflections in a simple indexed notebook (think M001, M002) so wisdom is easy to find later. A phrase like “My times are in your hands” becomes a prayer to focus on what you can control—your attitude and faith—while trusting God with outcomes.

Nourish Before You Perform

George Müller’s breakthrough was practical: make your soul happy in God before you try to serve Him. Reading and meditating on the Word is not a warm-up to work; it is the work that orders everything else.

Step Three: Pray Like a Person, Not a Pro

Prayer is a familiar, earnest conversation. If you need structure, use ACTS—Adoration, Confession, Thanksgiving, Supplication—beginning with who God is, not what you need. If your mind leaps to chores, write them down and return. Prefer spontaneity? Try the “ballroom” approach: let the Spirit lead topic by topic. Anchor requests to a promise from Scripture.

A Useful Shock

Asked how much prayer fueled his growing church, the speaker froze—he’d been too busy for it. The moment exposed a common temptation: letting work for God crowd out life with God. The remedy is not guilt, but a renewed habit.

From Dating to Marriage

Spiritual maturity isn’t chasing mountaintop moments; it’s showing up daily through the ups and downs. Think less mind-blowing encounter, more faithful companionship.

How to Begin Tomorrow

Try five minutes each: center your mind, read and record, then pray. Grow the time as it grows on you. Consistency beats intensity—and makes space for joy.

“The first great and primary business to which I had to attend every day was to have my soul happy in the Lord.” — George Müller
“Prayer is a familiar and earnest talking to God.” — John Knox
“Anybody can date God. The truly mature seeks to be his faithful lifelong companion.” — Gary Thomas
“Name it to tame it.”

Key Takeaways

  • Open the day by naming feelings, practicing gratitude, and reframing challenges.
  • Record Scripture insights—short notes in margins, longer reflections in an indexed journal.
  • Seek joy in God before doing for God; nourishment precedes performance.
  • Pray simply: start with adoration, confess honestly, give thanks, then ask boldly.
  • Write down distractions and add a biblical promise to your prayers.
