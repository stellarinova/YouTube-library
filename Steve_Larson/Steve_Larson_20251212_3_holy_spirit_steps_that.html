3 Holy Spirit Steps That TRANSFORM My Morning!

Channel: Steve Larson
Published: 20251212
If your mornings vanish into pings and to‑dos, you’re not alone. This simple three-step rhythm—center your mind, linger in Scripture, and pray like you talk—turns scattered starts into steady companionship with God.

Step 1: Center Your Mind

Begin with expressive writing. Name what you feel—anxiety, excitement, dread—because naming it tames it. A feelings wheel can help you find precise words. Then practice gratitude; specific is terrific. Reframe challenges by drafting five alternative interpretations of a hard moment and asking which aligns with God’s character. Close by reflecting on yesterday: What was most meaningful? What did you learn? What would you do differently?

Step 2: Read—and Write in—the Word

Let Scripture speak, then respond in ink. A wide-margin Bible invites brief margin notes; a notebook holds longer reflections indexed for easy return. The point isn’t pretty pages—it’s a dialog with God. Many find a steady rhythm in five Psalms and one Proverb a day, plus another passage. As George Mueller discovered, being with the Lord comes before doing for the Lord; Scripture nourishes a happy soul.

Step 3: Pray Like You Talk

John Knox called prayer “a familiar and earnest talking to God.” Keep it simple. Use the ACTS pattern when you need structure: Adoration (start with who God is), Confession (ask the Spirit to reveal what’s unholy), Thanksgiving (name gifts with specificity), and Supplication (ask for daily bread). Or follow Dick Eastman’s hour-by-hour prompts when time allows.

Staying Present in Prayer

When stray tasks pop up, write them down so your mind can return to God. Try two modes: structured tracks or “ballroom dancing,” letting the Spirit lead what to pray next. Anchor your requests to a promise—Psalm 81:10 was Mueller’s favorite—so desire is yoked to Scripture, not drift.

From Busy for God to With God

The temptation is real: serving can eclipse seeking. An interviewer once asked what role prayer played in a growing church—and the honest answer was, not enough. Gary Thomas offers a better aim: don’t chase a dazzling date with God; learn to be married to him through routine and rough patches. Start small—five minutes per step—and grow. Consistency is intimacy’s quiet engine.

Key Takeaways

  • Open with expressive writing, gratitude, reframing, and a brief daily examen.
  • Read Scripture with pen in hand; capture margin insights and index longer reflections.
  • Pray simply using ACTS, or follow the Spirit’s lead when structure isn’t needed.
  • Write down distractions; return your attention without guilt.
  • Attach promises to petitions to align prayer with God’s word.
  • Begin small—five minutes per step—and build a habit that lasts.
