3 Holy Spirit Steps That TRANSFORM My Morning!

Channel: Steve Larson
Published: 20251212
Busy lives and glowing screens make quiet time with God feel impossible. The remedy isn’t grit; it’s a simple, repeatable rhythm that settles the mind, feeds the soul, and opens a real conversation with God—before the day starts bossing you around.

Why Mornings Slip Away

We intend to pray, then wake up anxious, doom-scroll, and sprint into tasks. The fix begins with a surprising first move: tend your mind before you try to be spiritual. Clear the inner noise so you can actually hear.

Step 1: Center Your Mind

Start with expressive writing—two or three minutes naming what you feel. A “feeling wheel” (a simple list of emotions) helps you move beyond vague stress. Gratitude comes next: be specific because specific is terrific. Then reframe problems by generating five alternate interpretations and asking which aligns with Scripture. Close by reviewing yesterday: what mattered, what you learned, what you’d do differently.

Step 2: Read the Word, Write It Down

Use a wide‑margin Bible and jot short insights right beside the text. For longer reflections, keep a simple indexed notebook (M001, M002…) so ideas are easy to find later. A steady diet helps: five Psalms and one Proverb a day, plus other readings. The aim isn’t checking a box; it’s letting Scripture make your soul happy in God—being with the Lord before doing for the Lord.

A Simple Note on Tools

Pens that don’t bleed, a stencil, and light highlighters can make note‑taking a quiet pleasure. But the real tool is attention: capture one crisp takeaway per passage. Short truths in the margin, longer prayers or insights in the journal.

Step 3: Pray Simply, Pray Deeply

Prayer is familiar, earnest talk with God. Begin with adoration (who God is), then confession, thanksgiving, and finally requests (ACTS). If a to‑do pops up, write it down so it stops hijacking your focus. Prefer structure? Use a planned outline. Prefer spontaneity? Try “ballroom” prayer—let the Spirit lead. Either way, anchor requests to a promise from Scripture.

When Doing Replaces Being

Success can disguise a starving prayer life. The author recalls being asked how prayer fueled church growth—and freezing, because busyness had crowded out communion. The warning lands gently: never let the work of the Lord outrun time with the Lord of the work.

From Dating God to a Marriage

Chasing spiritual fireworks is easy. The mature path is steady companionship—showing up daily through highs, lows, and routine. Think covenant, not crush. Ten faithful minutes each morning beats occasional mountaintops.

Start Small, Stay Faithful

Try five minutes per step: mind, Scripture, prayer. Grow from there. Keep it human, consistent, and honest. The goal isn’t performance; it’s presence.

Key Takeaways

  • Three steps: center your mind, read and journal Scripture, then pray with focus.
  • Expressive writing, specific gratitude, reframing, and a brief daily review clear mental clutter.
  • Margin notes plus an indexed journal capture insights you’ll actually revisit.
  • Begin prayer with adoration; use ACTS or free‑flow, and write down distractions to stay present.
  • Attach requests to a biblical promise and prioritize presence with God over productivity for God.
