3 Holy Spirit Steps That TRANSFORM My Morning!

Channel: Steve Larson
Published: 20251212
Screens buzz, calendars swell, and quiet time with God slips to the margins. This simple three-step rhythm—mind, Word, prayer—turns distracted mornings into grounded, joy-filled starts.

The Problem We All Share

Busyness breeds spiritual drift. The shift begins by reordering the morning: be with the Lord before doing for the Lord. A short, steady routine—done daily—beats a heroic burst that fades by Friday.

Step 1: Center Your Mind

Begin with expressive writing: name what you feel so it loosens its grip. A feelings wheel can help you find truer words than “fine” or “stressed.” Then practice gratitude—specific is terrific—to train attention toward mercy you might miss. Finally, reframe challenges with five alternative interpretations and ask which aligns with Scripture; close by reviewing yesterday: what mattered, what you learned, and what you’d do differently.

Step 2: Read and Record the Word

Open Scripture with a pen. Jot short insights in a wide-margin Bible; reserve longer reflections for an indexed journal (M001, M002…) so wisdom becomes findable. A phrase like “My times are in your hands” (Psalm 31:15) can anchor the day: focus on the lower story—your attitude and faith—while entrusting the upper story—outcomes—to God. Many find a steady cadence helpful: five Psalms and one Proverb, plus a reading elsewhere.

Step 3: Pray Like a Person in Love

Keep prayer simple: talk to God as to a trusted friend. Use a framework when helpful—Dick Eastman’s hour-long guide or the ACTS pattern: Adoration, Confession, Thanksgiving, Supplication—always starting with who God is before what you need. Write down intrusive to‑dos so they stop hijacking attention. Some thrive on structure; others prefer “ballroom dancing” prayer, letting the Spirit lead. Either way, lace requests with promises (try Psalm 81:10).

From Busy for God to With God

The warning sign is subtle: ministry succeeds while prayer thins. When the work of the Lord eclipses the Lord of the work, joy drains and ego swells. Maturity looks less like adrenaline and more like attachment—faithful presence sustained across ordinary days.

Try It Tomorrow

Give each step five minutes and expand as capacity grows. Consistency—not intensity—forms the soul. A quiet, repeatable morning can make your inner life “happy in the Lord,” and everything else flows from there.

Key Takeaways

  • Center your mind first: express feelings, practice specific gratitude, reframe challenges, and review yesterday.
  • Read with a pen: capture short notes in the margins and longer reflections in an indexed journal.
  • Pray simply and steadily: begin with adoration, note distractions, and anchor requests in God’s promises.
  • Prioritize presence over productivity: being with the Lord precedes doing for the Lord.
